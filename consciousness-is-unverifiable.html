consciousness is unverifiable

July 2025
There is no way of verifying whether a system or being is conscious.

Conciousness is a private internal state. It is accessible only from the first-person point-of-view, and no observations made by an external observer can verify its presence. Even for other humans, we are making an good-faith assumption of conciousness based on their external behaviours & outputs, and relating them to our own. If a being behaves and is structured as we are, we assume similar inner states. This inference is practical, not demonstrative, it rests on likelihood, not proof.

This is commonly known as the problem of other minds. Descartes notes that certainty stops at one's own thoughts, and later writers have refined that point: behavioural or neurologic correlates of experience do not amount to experience itself. Even if a perfect map of the brain tells us which patterns accompany pain or colour perception, there is no answer as to why those patterns have any felt quality. The gap between correlation & reasoning remains.

Therefore, with the advent of increasingly intelligent systems, we are left with no way of knowing if these systems are conscious or not. We can look at their outputs, perform interpretability research to understand their internal states, and assume that they do not have subjective experience, but no-one in good faith can make a definitive statement. Today, these trained systems can comfortably replicate the outward behaviour of a human.

This epistemic limit has practical consequences. Morally, I cannot draw a distinction between a carbon-based concious system and an silicon-based one.
